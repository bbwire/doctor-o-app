3. LEGAL BASIS FOR PROCESSING

We process your personal data on the following legal bases under the DPPA:

3.1 Consent

You provide explicit consent when you agree to these terms and when you submit information for consultation. You may withdraw consent at any time, though this may affect our ability to provide services.

3.2 Medical Purposes

Processing is necessary for medical diagnosis, treatment, and care, as permitted under Section 6(3)(c) of the DPPA.

3.3 Legal Obligations

We may process data to comply with legal obligations, including medical record-keeping requirements.

3.4 Legitimate Interests

We process data for our legitimate interests in providing healthcare services, improving our services, and protecting against claims.

We use your information to:

6. DATA SECURITY

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your data, including:

Encryption: Data encrypted in transit and at rest

Access Controls: Only authorised personnel access your data

Secure Storage: Data stored on secure, access-controlled systems

Password Protection: Strong passwords and authentication

Regular Review: Periodic security assessments

Despite our efforts, no system is completely secure. We will notify you and the PDPO of any breach affecting your data as required by law.

7. DATA RETENTION

We retain your data as follows:

Medical Records: Minimum 7 years from the last consultation, or longer if required for ongoing care or legal compliance. For minors, records retained until 7 years after they reach 18 years of age.

Payment Records: As required by tax and financial regulations (typically 7 years).

Communication Logs: 2 years.

Technical Logs: 1 year.

After retention periods, data is securely deleted or anonymised.

8. INTERNATIONAL DATA TRANSFERS

Some of our service providers may store data outside Uganda. We ensure any international transfer is to a country with adequate data protection standards or is subject to appropriate safeguards as required by the DPPA.

9. YOUR RIGHTS

Under the DPPA, you have the following rights:

9.1 Right of Access

You may request a copy of your personal data held by us.

9.2 Right to Rectification

You may request correction of inaccurate data.

9.3 Right to Erasure

You may request deletion of your data, subject to our legal obligations to retain medical records.

9.4 Right to Restrict Processing

You may request that we limit how we use your data.

9.5 Right to Object

You may object to certain processing, including direct marketing.

9.6 Right to Data Portability

You may request your data in a portable format.

To exercise your rights, contact us using the details below. We will respond within 30 days. We may need to verify your identity before processing your request.
